November 2023 Edition

Editorial Note

Heggidira? How are you? This same question echoed in my mind at the recent SAATA conference, where I met many TA professionals, enthusiasts, and old pals. Their smiles, compliments, and chats made me feel like I was returning home after a long journey. We shared our insights and excitement, and I felt inspired, recharged, and connected.

If you were there with us at the conference, we want to express our heartfelt gratitude for your presence and participation. You made the conference a success with your enthusiasm and curiosity. You joined the varied workshops that were planned with care and thought, and you contributed to the learning and the fun.

If you missed the conference, don't worry. We have a short compilation of what it was like. You can read how our keynote speaker Karen Minikin felt as she spent two days with us in Bangalore. You can also read the President's note from Aruna Gopakumar, who was deeply involved in the conference and shares her thoughts with us. You can also find some experiences from our TA community members who attended the conference.

The event ended on a high note by celebrating those who cleared their exams recently. You can head to our Experiences Section to read more.

We have more for you in this edition of the newsletter. We have updates from our departments who work hard to make SAATA a place for networking and developing competencies in transactional analysis. We also have a creative corner with an amusing cartoon quip by Haider Jasdan and a book review by Asha Raghavan.

As I wrap up my note, I want to share with you that this question is a first step to small talk. Small talk can lead to big things, as our keynote speaker explained. I recommend a beautiful technique called ACT, which stands for Authentic Connections and Taste. Use it to converse with others while giving them a taste of who you are.

Dive in! Who knows where this conversation might lead?

Wishing you all a very happy and prosperous Diwali 2023 from the SAATA Newsletter Team.

Madhulika Rao
Managing Editor
