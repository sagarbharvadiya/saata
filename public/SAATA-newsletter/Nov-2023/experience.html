My Aha Moments of Diploma Writing

My journey through the exam writing experience has been profound, as it enriched me with discovering my own self. It was like reliving my past experiences that provided me with valuable insights about my strengths, challenges, and limitations.

Taking this opportunity to understand myself by writing about my journey, I grieved for my losses, came to peace with my struggles, and had a sense of serenity and tranquillity when I got absorbed in writing and time seemed to fly by.

The constant support from my supervisors in helping me reinvent my story added flavor and the final outcome felt divine. I felt proud of my willingness to discover myself and to emerge stronger through the entire process of self-awareness. I discovered that personal growth is an ongoing journey and every setback has provided me with an opportunity to grow and evolve.


Ritu Gupta

Diploma Training Experience

My TA Diploma foundation started with so much excitement. Felt like wow, finally the dream is coming true!

It was like going through a new discovery and I suddenly felt like I was in an operation theatre doing a post-mortem and forensic test on my own body and soul. Oh! There was so much pain and numbness embracing my body and mind. I was shattered and cried for a few months before starting to shift slowly little by little. I managed and coped with the help of my trainers. Working on myself created a new version of me. Although patterns come back unconsciously, now I am aware of that. Self-talk Chalu hain. Shhhhhh! behave like an Adult and feel like an Adult. My favorite place is free child and if triggered by anything, my angry Amitabh Bachchan comes out.

As I look back, I realise that so often I was guided by serendipity and the words I chanced upon in my early training notes as a PTSTA - “Change occurs when you become who you are and not try to become what you are not”.    Both the time of preparation and the exam was stimulating, challenging, affirming and a fulfilling experience.  

What can I say when I was able to overcome my wounds - sorrow, grief, failure, and sadness. I don't know about passing or failing. I learned and am keen to learn more about TA.

My heartfelt gratitude to dear Suriya Sir and Mohan Sir.

Tanzim Noor - Trainee
Bangladesh
