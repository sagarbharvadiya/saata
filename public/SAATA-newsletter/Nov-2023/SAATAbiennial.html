From Deepa’s Diary: Core Committee Volunteer of
SAATA Conference Experience.

Though I agree that the conference had some amazing workshops, presentations, and panel discussions, another group that was holding conversations of the same richness was the Steering Committee. We were a vibrant bunch of 12 committee members, convened by Aruna Gopakumar, who worked together for the conference. Some of the biggies of SAATA like Suriyaprakash C, Ragini Rao, Gunjan Zutshi, Parul Arora, and Deepak Dhananjaya lead the first timers - Devika Khanna, John Leo, Nisha George, Shibani Priyadarshini, Manish Narayan and myself through the whole process.

We met diligently every Monday on Zoom, for the entire planning period of 6 months which started in mid-February. There was approximately 80% attendance in all our meetings despite other pressing commitments.

Plenty of thought went into designing the workshops, inviting the speakers, and bringing relevant keynote speakers. Many meetings were spent designing the entire program, and discussing the placement of workshops and paper presentations - all needing to be compressed within two days. We spent countless hours coordinating with multiple dealers and suppliers, which turned out to be smooth at times, but frustrating during others. The entertaining performance led by Anagha Karvir and her band, and the sound healing session on the morning of the second day by Drumbeats India, were actually last-minute additions. However, they brought a lot of fun, celebration, and peace to the whole congregation.

My most memorable moments are those during the last month of planning. We were thrilled when we hit the required number of registrations. Till then, we brainstormed numerous ways to increase our visibility. We even formed a small group for outreach which included Ramya Navin, Deepak Sharma, and me, led by Deepak Dhananjaya. Together we reached out to different NGOs, colleges, and anyone else we could think of, to be a part of the conference.

When we scanned the venue a couple of weeks before the big day, we felt the absolute thrill and reality of all of it coming together. Being restricted to meeting online, we experienced immense joy in meeting the committee members in person. Excited, thrilled, and with broad smiles stretching all over our faces, we felt our committee meetings facilitated connections and brought many of us closer during the conference. The lunch and coffee breaks gave us ample time to pose with each other and the Heggidera selfie cut out.

All of us received positive responses from the attendees. We were humbled to hear them appreciate the lineup of amazing workshops, the depth brought out by Karen Minikin’s keynote, and the richness of the conference. I felt overwhelmed by the appreciation and gratitude around me. Our hearts were full of pride when we saw the breaks fully utilized by many for networking, hanging out with their buddies from the TA community, exploring new avenues together with plenty of smiles and hugs.

As newcomers to the committee, we felt warmth and joy in being included in key decision- making. We had the freedom to explore new territories without being restricted by any rigid rules. We felt a sense of belonging, where our opinion mattered. We were welcomed with open arms and groomed with gentleness. I am grateful to be a part of such a loving community.

Deepa Kumar,
TA Trainee
